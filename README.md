# An Academic View of Financial Research - Quantomics

**A Systematic Analysis of 75 Years of Foundational Research (1950-2024)**

This repository represents an independent research project analyzing 108 seminal papers in financial econometrics, organized into 8 progressive learning phases with 25+ thematic classifications. The compendium maps the intellectual genealogy of quantitative finance, from classical time series diagnostics through modern machine learning applications.

## üìö The 8-Phase Learning Architecture

### Phase 1: Foundations (Papers 1-20) 
**Focus**: Classical Econometrics & Testing

Master regression diagnostics, time series fundamentals, heteroskedasticity testing, structural stability, and advanced estimation methods. Essential building blocks before any advanced work.

**Key Papers**: Durbin-Watson (1950-51), Dickey-Fuller (1979-81), White (1980), Chow (1960)

---

### Phase 2: The Volatility Revolution (Papers 21-40) 
**Focus**: ARCH/GARCH and Volatility Modeling

The breakthrough that transformed finance. Study chronologically from Engle's original ARCH through GARCH extensions, realized volatility, and regime-switching models.

**Key Papers**: 
- **Engle (1982)** - ARCH: The paper that changed everything
- **Bollerslev (1986)** - GARCH: Extended the revolution
- **Hamilton (1989)** - Regime Switching: New paradigm

**Thematic Coverage**: ARCH/GARCH family, stochastic volatility, realized volatility, regime-switching models

---

### Phase 3: Asset Pricing Theory (Papers 41-62) 
**Focus**: How Assets Are Priced

From Black-Scholes option pricing through factor models and return predictability. Understand both theoretical foundations and empirical reality checks.

**Key Papers**:
- **Black & Scholes (1973)** - Nobel Prize winning option pricing
- **Fama & French (1992)** - Cross-section: Changed asset pricing forever
- **Goyal & Welch (2008)** - Reality check on predictability

**Thematic Coverage**: Option pricing, factor models, return predictability, empirical asset pricing

---

### Phase 4: Market Behavior (Papers 63-76) 
**Focus**: How Markets Actually Work

Bridge theory and reality. Market efficiency, technical trading, behavioral finance, and microstructure. Understand what works, what doesn't, and why.

**Key Papers**:
- **Mandelbrot (1963)** - Speculative price variation
- **Hong & Stein (1999)** - Unified behavioral theory
- **Malkiel (2003)** - EMH comprehensive survey

**Thematic Coverage**: Market efficiency, technical trading, behavioral biases, microstructure

---

### Phase 5: Macroeconomics & Markets (Papers 77-88) 
**Focus**: Macro-Financial Linkages

Connect macroeconomic forces to financial markets. VAR models, uncertainty shocks, monetary policy, and international transmission.

**Key Papers**:
- **Sims (1980)** - VAR: Seminal macro econometrics
- **Bloom (2009)** - Uncertainty shocks impact
- **Baker et al. (2016)** - Economic policy uncertainty

**Thematic Coverage**: VAR models, macro predictors, monetary policy, international finance

---

### Phase 6: Contagion & Crises (Papers 89-96) 
**Focus**: Financial Crises and Spillovers

How shocks propagate through the financial system. Network approaches, spillover analysis, and crisis dynamics.

**Key Papers**:
- **Diebold & Yilmaz (2012)** - Spillovers: Network revolution
- **A√Øt-Sahalia et al. (2015)** - Mutually exciting jumps
- **He et al. (2022)** - COVID-19 treasury crisis

**Thematic Coverage**: Contagion theory, network analysis, crisis liquidity

---

### Phase 7: Corporate Finance & Credit (Papers 97-101) 
**Focus**: Corporate Applications

Corporate financial decisions, credit risk, and derivatives markets. Connecting firm-level behavior to market-level phenomena.

**Thematic Coverage**: Capital structure, corporate governance, credit derivatives

---

### Phase 8: Machine Learning Era (Papers 102-108) 
**Focus**: AI in Finance

From random forests to deep learning. How machine learning is transforming empirical asset pricing and forecasting.

**Key Papers**:
- **Breiman (2001)** - Random Forests: ML classic
- **Gu, Kelly & Xiu (2020)** - ML Asset Pricing: Comprehensive study
- **Fischer & Krauss (2018)** - Deep LSTM for markets

**Thematic Coverage**: Random forests, deep learning, LSTM networks, ML regime detection
